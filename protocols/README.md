# Protocols

Replicable, evidence-based protocols derived from the case studies in this repository.

## What these protocols are

Each protocol here is a **generalization of what worked in a published case study**, structured so another person can replicate it (or adapt it with their practitioner) and track their own results.

The protocols are designed to be:

- **Concrete and time-boxed** — specific actions, specific durations, specific tracking points
- **Practitioner-compatible** — meant to be reviewed by a functional medicine practitioner, OBGYN, or REI who knows your history
- **Honest about evidence** — every recommendation traces back to either (a) the personal case study or (b) peer-reviewed research in `research/`
- **Modular** — start with the lowest-friction pillars; layer in the more advanced ones only as appropriate
- **Audience-aware** — three of the protocol's entry points are tuned for people preparing for surgery, recovering from surgery, and trying to avoid surgery

## What these protocols are NOT

- ❌ **Not a substitute for medical care.** They are educational scaffolding for conversations with your practitioner.
- ❌ **Not a prescription.** Supplement names and approximate doses are referenced for context only. Your practitioner determines what's right for you.
- ❌ **Not "one-size-fits-all".** They are derived from one person's response. Your physiology will differ.
- ❌ **Not a replacement for surgery.** For severe disease (deep infiltrating endometriosis, large ovarian endometriomas, certain adenomyosis presentations), surgery may be the right intervention regardless of how well a protocol reduces inflammation.

## ⚠️ Important: developed without hormonal contraception

All protocols here were developed **without** hormonal contraception, hormonal endometriosis suppression, or HRT. If you are currently on hormonal medication, **do not stop it without medical supervision**, and **review the protocol's supplement list with your prescriber** — a few supplements (DIM, calcium-D-glucarate, sulforaphane extracts, St. John's Wort) can theoretically alter synthetic hormone levels via the CYP450 enzyme system. See [`disclosures.md`](../disclosures.md) and [`../research/birth-control-context.md`](../research/birth-control-context.md).

## How to use a protocol

1. **Read the case study first.** Understand the personal context the protocol came from — what was working, what wasn't, what the data looked like. The case study is at the start of each protocol.
2. **Read the "why this works" section.** Understand the biochemical mechanism so you can have an informed conversation with your practitioner.
3. **Pick your audience entry point.** Each protocol has multiple framings depending on your specific situation (pre-surgery, post-surgery, surgery-hesitant, general root-cause).
4. **Talk to your practitioner before starting.** Bring the protocol document. Ask which pillars are appropriate for you, which supplements they want to adjust or omit, and what labs they want to track.
5. **Track your data.** Use the templates in `tracking/` to log symptoms, sleep, wearable metrics, and any lab markers your practitioner orders.
6. **Iterate honestly.** What worked, what didn't, what would you change. The case-study series exists because n=1 stories — your story — are how this kind of knowledge actually gets built.
